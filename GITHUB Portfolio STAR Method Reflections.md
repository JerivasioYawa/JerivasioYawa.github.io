# GITHUB Portfolio STAR Method Reflections

## 1. Reflection on Resume Writing

**Situation:**  
As an aspiring software developer, I was required to construct a professional resume that would effectively market my academic background, technical skills, and achievements to prospective employers within the IT industry.

**Task:**  
The task required consolidating all relevant information into a single cohesive document. This included selecting a suitable professional template, curating content strategically, and ensuring formatting met industry standards without overcrowding the document.

**Action:**  
I approached the process with careful attention to detail, critically assessing which achievements and skills were most relevant to software development roles. Each decision was guided by the goal of producing a competitive and viable document. The process demanded both honest self-assessment and strategic thinking in terms of personal brand building.

**Result:**  
The outcome was a well-structured, professionally formatted resume that effectively communicates my value as an emerging software engineering professional and serves as a functional personal marketing tool for future job applications.

## 2. Reflection on Mock Interview

**Situation:**  
I was required to participate in a mock interview simulating a real-world professional interview for a software developer role, designed to assess and develop my overall interview readiness.

**Task:**  
The task was to respond to industry-aligned interview questions using the STAR framework, while engaging in both internal and external constructive feedback to identify strengths and areas requiring further development.

**Action:**  
I selected questions frequently encountered in real-world IT interviews to ensure authenticity. I applied the STAR method to structure my responses while critically reflecting on both my verbal strengths and the decline in my body language due to performance anxiety as the interview progressed.

**Result:**  
The mock interview produced valuable and directly applicable insights. It confirmed verbal self-presentation as a reliable strength while identifying non-verbal communication as a key development area to be improved through repeated mock sessions and deliberate self-review.

## 3. Reflection on GitHub Pages

**Situation:**  
A platform was required to publicly display and showcase my portfolio, including my resume and mock interview video, as part of building a professional online presence as an aspiring software developer.

**Task:**  
The task involved setting up and deploying GitHub Pages to host my portfolio content in a professional and accessible manner, while expanding my practical understanding of GitHub beyond its use as a code repository.

**Action:**  
I followed the provided setup documentation, which was clear and well-structured. This allowed me to navigate the configuration process with minimal difficulty while engaging with key concepts such as repository management, committing changes, and linking content to a live environment.

**Result:**  
My portfolio was successfully deployed and is now publicly accessible via my GitHub Pages link. This experience broadened my understanding of GitHub as a professional showcase tool and strengthened my familiarity with industry-relevant platforms as an emerging software engineering professional.
